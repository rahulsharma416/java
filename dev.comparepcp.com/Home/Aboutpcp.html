What is PCP?

Personal Contract Purchase (PCP) is a popular vehicle financing option as it offers fixed monthly repayments and the ability to change your car every few years.

Pros :

  • You get an option to buy or return the car at the end of your agreement.
  • Discounts : Usually manufacturers promote PCP with discounts known as "Deposit Contribution" or "Finance allowance". It can range from 0% to 25% of the price of the car. This helps you save more.

Cons : (If you don’t buy the car at the end of your agreement)

  • Excess mileage charge is applicable if you have driven the car more than agreed mileage.
  • Damage charge.

How does it work?

0. Understand

Before opting for PCP product, you must ensure that you have understood the product clearly. Ask the relevant dealer to explain you the product in detail. Following are few important points you must discuss in detail.

  • Excess mileage charge : If you drive beyond the agreed mileage and if you decide to return/swap the car at the end of agreement, an excess mileage charge shall be applicable.
  • Damage charge : If the car is damaged beyond fair wear and tear, then a charge shall be applicable.
  • Refer to all other details like APR%, Fixed Rate, Final Payment, Option to purchase fee and most important T&Cs.

How does it work?

1. Initial deposit

You may have to pay an initial deposit, a higher deposit may result in lower monthly payments. There could be a maximum limit on deposit. You can pay deposit either in cash, by part exchanging your existing car or a combination of both.

How does it work?

2. N equal monthly payments

"N" will depend on the duration of the agreement. E.g. For 36 months agreement, you will have to pay 35 equal monthly payments.

So every month, you will have to pay a fix payment.

How does it work?

3. At the end, you will have 3 options

  • Option 1 : Hand the car back to the dealer and walk away (subject to vehicle damage and excess mileage charges)
  • Option 2 : Swap your car for a new model and renew your PCP contract (Depending on equity you have in the car)
  • Option 3 : Buy the car outright by paying off the balloon payment (the depreciated value of the car) and option to purchase fee.
